The Kalamazoo County Criminal Justice System

Kalamazoo County is a major regional center in Southwest Michigan, home to approximately 260,000 residents. The city of Kalamazoo serves as the county seat and hosts multiple universities including Western Michigan University and Kalamazoo College. This creates a unique demographic mix of permanent residents, college students, and transient populations that shapes the county's criminal justice landscape.

The county's criminal courts process thousands of cases annually, ranging from student-related alcohol offenses to serious violent felonies. Major highways including I-94 and US-131 run through the county, leading to significant traffic enforcement and drug interdiction efforts. Understanding the complexities of Kalamazoo County's criminal justice system is essential when facing charges here.

Kalamazoo County Courts: Where Your Case Will Be Heard

Criminal cases in Kalamazoo County are handled through two distinct court systems:

9th Circuit Court - Kalamazoo

Address: Kalamazoo County Courthouse, 227 W Michigan Avenue, Kalamazoo, MI 49007

The 9th Circuit Court has exclusive jurisdiction over all felony cases in Kalamazoo County. This is where serious criminal charges are prosecuted and tried before juries. The court handles cases including armed robbery, criminal sexual conduct, drug trafficking, assault with intent to do great bodily harm, home invasion, and all other felony offenses. Our trial attorneys have experience defending clients in the 9th Circuit Court and understand the expectations and procedures of this courthouse.

8th District Court - Two Locations

The 8th District Court serves Kalamazoo County with two divisions:

Kalamazoo Division: 227 W Michigan Avenue, Kalamazoo, MI 49007

Portage Division: 7900 Shaver Road, Portage, MI 49024

The 8th District Court handles all misdemeanor criminal cases from arraignment through trial, as well as the preliminary proceedings for felony cases including arraignments and preliminary examinations. Depending on where you were arrested, your case will be heard in either the Kalamazoo or Portage division. We regularly appear in both courthouses and are familiar with the judges, prosecutors, and procedures at each location.

Common Criminal Charges in Kalamazoo County

Kalamazoo County's urban environment, university populations, and major highway corridors result in distinct patterns of criminal charges:

  • OWI/DUI: Operating while intoxicated charges are extremely common, particularly in areas near Western Michigan University and downtown Kalamazoo entertainment districts. We defend all levels of OWI charges including first offense, second offense, high BAC (.17 or above), and felony third offense.
  • Drug Offenses: From simple possession to delivery and manufacturing, Kalamazoo County sees significant drug enforcement. Charges commonly involve marijuana, cocaine, methamphetamine, heroin, fentanyl, MDMA, and prescription drugs. Interstate highway traffic through the county results in many drug interdiction cases.
  • Domestic Violence: Domestic assault charges are prosecuted aggressively in Kalamazoo County. The prosecutor's office takes these cases seriously regardless of whether the alleged victim wants to proceed with charges.
  • Assault Crimes: Assault and battery, aggravated assault, assault with a dangerous weapon, and assault causing serious injury. Bar fights and other altercations near campus and entertainment districts generate many assault charges.
  • Retail Fraud: Shoplifting charges from the many retail centers in Portage, Kalamazoo, and throughout the county.
  • Minor in Possession (MIP): Alcohol violations involving university students and minors.
  • Disorderly Conduct and Disturbing the Peace: Often related to nightlife and campus activities.
  • Property Crimes: Breaking and entering, larceny, motor vehicle theft, and receiving stolen property.
  • Traffic Offenses: Driving while license suspended, reckless driving, and fleeing and eluding police.

Law Enforcement Agencies in Kalamazoo County

Criminal charges in Kalamazoo County can result from arrests by multiple law enforcement agencies:

  • Kalamazoo County Sheriff's Office: Provides law enforcement services to townships and operates the county jail
  • Kalamazoo Department of Public Safety: Combined police and fire services for the City of Kalamazoo
  • Portage Department of Public Safety: Serves the City of Portage
  • Western Michigan University Department of Public Safety: University police with jurisdiction on campus and adjacent areas
  • Parchment Police Department
  • Michigan State Police: Very active on I-94 and US-131 conducting traffic enforcement and drug interdiction
  • Southwest Enforcement Team (SWET): Multi-agency drug task force operating in Southwest Michigan

Each agency has different enforcement priorities and practices. Our experience with these agencies helps us evaluate the evidence and identify potential defenses in your case.

Special Considerations for Kalamazoo County Cases

Certain factors make Kalamazoo County criminal cases unique:

University Student Defendants: Criminal convictions can affect financial aid, housing, and future career prospects. We work to achieve outcomes that minimize these collateral consequences when possible.

Interstate Drug Cases: I-94 serves as a major drug trafficking corridor. Kalamazoo County sees many cases involving non-residents stopped on the highway. These cases often involve complex Fourth Amendment search and seizure issues that require experienced criminal defense counsel.

Urban Crime Patterns: The city of Kalamazoo faces urban crime challenges including gang activity, gun violence, and drug trafficking. Cases arising in the city may be charged more seriously than similar conduct in outlying areas.

Multiple Jurisdictions: With two district court divisions, understanding where your case will be heard and the procedural differences between locations matters for case strategy.
