Common Criminal Charges in Holland, MI

We defend Holland residents against all types of criminal charges, including:

DUI / OWI Charges

Holland police conduct frequent traffic stops and DUI checkpoints, especially along major corridors like US-31, River Avenue, and downtown during Tulip Time and summer festivals. If you've been arrested for drunk driving in Holland, we can challenge breathalyzer results, field sobriety tests, and police procedures. Learn more about our DUI defense services.

Domestic Violence

Domestic violence arrests are common in Holland, and police are required to arrest someone whenever they're called to a domestic disturbance. Even if no one was injured and the alleged victim wants to drop charges, prosecutors often proceed anyway. We defend against false accusations, exaggerated claims, and situations where you were acting in self-defense. Learn more about domestic violence defense.

Drug Charges

Ottawa County takes drug offenses seriously. Whether you're facing possession of marijuana, cocaine, methamphetamine, prescription drugs, or more serious charges like possession with intent to deliver, we provide aggressive defense. Many drug cases can be resolved through diversion programs or reduced charges.

Theft & Retail Fraud

Retail fraud (shoplifting) charges are prosecuted aggressively in Holland, with stores like Meijer, Walmart, and downtown retailers actively pursuing charges. Even first-time offenders face criminal records. We can often negotiate dismissals or reduce charges to avoid a theft conviction that will follow you for life.

Assault & Battery

Bar fights, disputes at Holland events, and altercations downtown can result in assault charges. We defend clients by demonstrating self-defense, challenging witness credibility, and negotiating reduced charges.

Traffic Offenses

Reckless driving, driving while license suspended (DWLS), leaving the scene of an accident, and other serious traffic offenses can result in jail time, license suspension, and a criminal record. We help Holland drivers protect their licenses and avoid convictions.

What to Do If You're Arrested in Holland

If Holland police arrest you, follow these steps:

  1. Remain silent. Do NOT talk to police without a lawyer present. Invoke your right to remain silent and request an attorney immediately.
  2. Do not consent to searches. Police may ask to search your car, phone, or home. Politely decline and say you do not consent to searches.
  3. Call an attorney immediately. The sooner we start working on your case, the better. Call us at (616) 227-3303.
  4. Do not post about your case on social media. Prosecutors will review your Facebook, Instagram, and other accounts looking for evidence against you.
  5. Document everything. Write down what happened while it's fresh in your mind, including names of witnesses, photos of injuries, and any evidence supporting your defense.
