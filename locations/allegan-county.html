Understanding Allegan County's Criminal Justice System

Allegan County is a diverse region covering over 1,800 square miles in southwestern Michigan. From the lakeshore resort communities of Saugatuck and Douglas to the agricultural townships and the city of Allegan, the county serves approximately 120,000 residents. This mix of seasonal tourism, agricultural economy, and permanent residential communities creates unique law enforcement patterns and criminal defense challenges.

The county's criminal justice system handles everything from tourist-related OWI arrests during the busy summer season to drug offenses, domestic violence cases, and property crimes throughout the year. Understanding the local dynamics of Allegan County law enforcement and court procedures is essential for mounting an effective defense.

Allegan County Courts: Where Your Case Will Be Heard

Your criminal case in Allegan County will be handled in one of two courts, depending on the severity of your charges:

48th Circuit Court - Allegan

Address: 113 Chestnut Street, Allegan, MI 49010

The 48th Circuit Court is the trial court for all felony cases in Allegan County. Located in the county seat of Allegan, this court handles serious criminal charges including drug trafficking, criminal sexual conduct, assault with intent to do great bodily harm, home invasion, and other felonies. If you're charged with a felony, your case will ultimately be tried before a jury in this courthouse. Our trial lawyers have defended numerous clients in the 48th Circuit Court and understand how to effectively present a defense to Allegan County juries.

57th District Court - Allegan

Address: 113 Chestnut Street, Allegan, MI 49010

The 57th District Court shares the same courthouse building as the Circuit Court and handles all misdemeanor criminal cases from start to finish, as well as the preliminary stages of felony cases including arraignments and preliminary examinations. Whether you're charged with OWI, domestic violence, retail fraud, or any other misdemeanor, your case will be heard in this court. We regularly appear in the 57th District Court and have developed strong working relationships while maintaining our aggressive defense advocacy.

Common Criminal Charges in Allegan County

Allegan County's unique geographic and demographic characteristics result in certain types of criminal charges being particularly common:

  • OWI/DUI Offenses: The county's lakeshore resort communities attract significant summer tourism, leading to increased drunk driving arrests during peak season. Law enforcement agencies patrol heavily along Blue Star Highway and other major routes. We defend all OWI charges including first offense, second offense, high BAC, and operating while visibly impaired.
  • Drug Crimes: Allegan County has seen increased drug enforcement targeting methamphetamine, cocaine, heroin, and prescription drug offenses. Charges range from simple possession to delivery and manufacturing.
  • Domestic Violence: Like many rural counties, Allegan County takes domestic violence charges very seriously. Even first-time offenders face potential jail time, mandatory classes, and other consequences.
  • Retail Fraud/Shoplifting: Charges from stores in Plainwell, Otsego, Allegan, and the lakeshore communities.
  • Assault & Battery: Simple assault, aggravated assault, and assault with a dangerous weapon charges.
  • Property Crimes: Breaking and entering, larceny, and malicious destruction of property.
  • Traffic Violations: Driving while license suspended (DWLS), reckless driving, and other serious traffic offenses.
  • Weapons Charges: Carrying concealed weapons and other firearms violations.

Law Enforcement in Allegan County

Criminal charges in Allegan County can originate from arrests by various law enforcement agencies:

  • Allegan County Sheriff's Office: Primary law enforcement for townships and unincorporated areas
  • Allegan Department of Public Safety: Serves the City of Allegan
  • Plainwell Department of Public Safety
  • Otsego Police Department
  • Saugatuck-Douglas Police Department: Serves both lakeshore communities
  • Wayland Police Department
  • Michigan State Police: Active on US-131, I-196, M-40, M-89, and other major highways
  • West Michigan Enforcement Team (WEMET): Multi-jurisdictional drug task force

Each agency operates differently, and understanding their arrest patterns and evidence collection procedures can be critical to your defense. Our experience with Allegan County law enforcement helps us identify potential weaknesses in the prosecution's case.

Unique Challenges in Allegan County Cases

Allegan County's characteristics create some unique defense challenges:

Seasonal Tourism Impact: During summer months, the lakeshore communities see a dramatic increase in population and law enforcement activity. Tourist-related arrests, particularly OWI offenses, spike during this period. Non-residents facing charges may need special assistance navigating the court system from a distance.

Rural vs. Resort Dynamics: Cases arising in the agricultural townships may be viewed differently than those from resort communities like Saugatuck. Understanding these local perspectives is important for jury selection and case presentation.

Limited Public Transportation: Court-ordered requirements like substance abuse treatment or community service can be challenging for defendants without reliable transportation in this largely rural county. We work to address these practical considerations in plea negotiations and sentencing.
