Van Buren County's Criminal Justice System

Van Buren County is located in Southwest Michigan with approximately 76,000 residents. The county spans from the Lake Michigan shoreline through agricultural regions to its eastern border. Major communities include Paw Paw (the county seat), South Haven (a popular resort community), Hartford, Bangor, and Lawton. This mix of tourism, agriculture, and permanent residential communities creates diverse law enforcement patterns and criminal defense challenges.

I-196 and several state highways traverse the county, resulting in significant traffic enforcement and drug interdiction activity. The lakeshore resort area of South Haven sees increased population and law enforcement during summer months, while the agricultural regions have their own unique enforcement patterns. Understanding these local dynamics is essential for effective criminal defense in Van Buren County.

Van Buren County Courts: Where Your Case Will Be Heard

Criminal proceedings in Van Buren County take place in two court systems depending on the nature and severity of the charges:

36th Circuit Court - Paw Paw

Address: Van Buren County Courthouse, 212 Paw Paw Street, Paw Paw, MI 49079

The 36th Circuit Court has jurisdiction over all felony cases in Van Buren County. This court handles serious criminal charges including drug trafficking, criminal sexual conduct, armed robbery, assault with intent to do great bodily harm, home invasion, and all other felony offenses. If your case involves felony charges, it will ultimately be tried before a jury in this courthouse. Our trial lawyers have experience defending clients in the 36th Circuit Court and understand the expectations of Van Buren County juries and judges.

7th District Court - Two Locations

The 7th District Court serves Van Buren County with two divisions:

Paw Paw Division: 212 Paw Paw Street, Paw Paw, MI 49079

South Haven Division: 515 Williams Street, South Haven, MI 49090

The 7th District Court handles all misdemeanor criminal cases from beginning to end, as well as the preliminary stages of felony cases including arraignments and preliminary examinations. Depending on where you were arrested in Van Buren County, your case will be heard in either the Paw Paw or South Haven division. We regularly practice in both locations and understand the procedural differences and judicial preferences at each courthouse.

Common Criminal Charges in Van Buren County

Van Buren County's geographic and demographic characteristics result in specific patterns of criminal charges:

  • OWI/DUI Offenses: Operating while intoxicated charges are common, particularly during summer months in the South Haven resort area and along I-196. We defend all OWI charges including first offense, second offense, high BAC (.17 or above), and felony third offense cases.
  • Drug Crimes: Van Buren County sees significant drug enforcement activity. Common charges include possession, delivery, and manufacturing of methamphetamine, cocaine, heroin, fentanyl, and prescription drugs. Interstate highway traffic results in drug interdiction cases.
  • Domestic Violence: These charges are prosecuted seriously in Van Buren County. Even first-time offenders face potential jail time, probation, protective orders, and mandatory counseling.
  • Retail Fraud/Shoplifting: Theft charges from stores in Paw Paw, South Haven, and throughout the county.
  • Assault & Battery: Simple assault, aggravated assault, and assault with a dangerous weapon charges.
  • Property Crimes: Breaking and entering, larceny, and malicious destruction of property.
  • Traffic Violations: Driving while license suspended (DWLS), reckless driving, and fleeing and eluding.
  • Minor in Possession (MIP): Underage alcohol violations, particularly in the South Haven area.

Law Enforcement Agencies in Van Buren County

Criminal charges in Van Buren County can result from arrests by various law enforcement agencies:

  • Van Buren County Sheriff's Office: Primary law enforcement for townships and unincorporated areas
  • South Haven Department of Public Safety: Combined police and fire services for South Haven
  • Paw Paw Police Department
  • Hartford Police Department
  • Bangor Police Department
  • Michigan State Police: Active on I-196, M-40, and other major highways
  • Southwest Enforcement Team (SWET): Regional drug task force

Each agency has different enforcement priorities and practices. Understanding which agency arrested you can inform our defense strategy.

Unique Challenges in Van Buren County Cases

Several factors create unique challenges for criminal defendants in Van Buren County:

Rural Character: Van Buren County maintains a small-town character where news travels quickly. Local reputation can affect jury selection and case negotiations in ways that differ from larger, more urban counties.

Interstate Highway Cases: I-196 serves as a corridor for drug trafficking and results in many cases involving non-residents stopped by Michigan State Police. These cases often involve complex search and seizure issues.

Tourist vs. Local Dynamics: Cases involving tourists in South Haven may be handled differently than those involving permanent residents, creating different strategic considerations.
