What to Do If You're Arrested in Grand Haven

If Grand Haven police, Ottawa County Sheriff's deputies, or Michigan State Police arrest you, follow these critical steps:

  1. Remain silent. Do NOT talk to police without a lawyer present. Invoke your right to remain silent immediately: "I am invoking my right to remain silent, and I want to speak with an attorney."
  2. Do not consent to searches. Police may ask to search your car, phone, hotel room, or belongings. Politely say, "I do not consent to any searches."
  3. Do not resist arrest. Even if you believe the arrest is unjust, do not physically resist. Resisting arrest is an additional criminal charge that makes your case significantly worse.
  4. Call an attorney immediately. The sooner we start working on your case, the better. Call us at (616) 227-3303 as soon as possible.
  5. Document everything. Write down what happened, names of witnesses, officer badge numbers, and any evidence supporting your defense. Take photos of injuries, property damage, or anything relevant to your case.
  6. Do not post about your case on social media. Prosecutors will review your Facebook, Instagram, and other social media accounts looking for evidence against you. Anything you post can and will be used against you.
  7. Attend all court dates. Missing court dates results in bench warrants. If you can't make a court date, call us immediately to request an adjournment.
