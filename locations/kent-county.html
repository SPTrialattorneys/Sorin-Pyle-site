Navigating Kent County's Complex Criminal Justice System

Kent County is Michigan's fourth-largest county and home to Grand Rapids, the state's second-largest city. With a population exceeding 650,000 residents, Kent County's criminal justice system is one of the busiest in West Michigan. The county processes thousands of criminal cases annually, ranging from minor misdemeanors to serious violent felonies.

The complexity of Kent County's court system, with multiple district courts serving different municipalities, requires defense attorneys who are intimately familiar with the procedures, personnel, and local practices across all jurisdictions. Each courthouse has its own culture, and prosecutors may approach cases differently depending on where charges are filed. This local knowledge can make a critical difference in the outcome of your case.

Kent County Courts: Where Your Case Will Be Heard

Kent County's court system includes one Circuit Court for felonies and multiple District Courts for misdemeanors and preliminary proceedings:

17th Circuit Court - Grand Rapids

Address: Kent County Courthouse, 180 Ottawa Avenue NW, Grand Rapids, MI 49503

The 17th Circuit Court has exclusive jurisdiction over all felony cases in Kent County. This is where serious criminal charges are tried, including armed robbery, criminal sexual conduct, drug trafficking, home invasion, and other felonies. The 17th Circuit is one of Michigan's largest and busiest trial courts. Our trial attorneys have extensive experience defending clients before Kent County juries and understand the strategies that work in this courthouse.

61st District Court - Grand Rapids

Address: 180 Ottawa Avenue NW, Grand Rapids, MI 49503

The 61st District Court serves the City of Grand Rapids and handles all misdemeanor cases arising within the city limits, as well as the initial stages of all felony cases including arraignments and preliminary examinations. This is one of the busiest district courts in Michigan, processing thousands of OWI, domestic violence, assault, and other misdemeanor cases each year. We appear in this court regularly and have built strong working relationships while maintaining our commitment to aggressive client advocacy.

63rd District Court - Grand Rapids (Divisions A & B)

Division A Address: 2922 Fuller Avenue NE, Grand Rapids, MI 49505

Division B Address: 4240 Kalamazoo Avenue SE, Grand Rapids, MI 49508

The 63rd District Court has two divisions serving different areas of Kent County outside the City of Grand Rapids. We regularly practice in both divisions and understand the procedural differences between them.

62-A District Court - Wyoming

Address: 1332 Galewood Avenue SW, Wyoming, MI 49509

Serving the City of Wyoming, this court handles misdemeanor cases and felony preliminary proceedings for one of Kent County's largest suburbs. Wyoming law enforcement is particularly active in OWI and drug enforcement.

62-B District Court - Kentwood

Address: 4740 Walma Avenue SE, Kentwood, MI 49512

The Kentwood District Court serves the City of Kentwood and processes a high volume of traffic offenses, OWI cases, and retail fraud charges from the area's many shopping centers.

59th District Court - Walker & Grandville

Walker Division: 4243 Lake Michigan Drive NW, Walker, MI 49534

Grandville Division: 3195 Wilson Avenue SW, Grandville, MI 49418

The 59th District Court operates two divisions serving the cities of Walker and Grandville, as well as several surrounding townships. We represent clients in both locations.

Common Criminal Charges in Kent County

Kent County's urban environment, major highways, and large population create unique law enforcement priorities. Common charges we defend include:

  • OWI/DUI: Kent County law enforcement agencies conduct aggressive drunk driving enforcement, including regular sobriety checkpoints on major roads. We defend first offense OWI, high BAC cases, second offense OWI, and felony third offense cases.
  • Domestic Violence: Domestic assault charges are prosecuted vigorously in Kent County. The prosecutor's office has a specialized domestic violence unit that handles these cases.
  • Drug Offenses: From simple possession to large-scale trafficking, Kent County sees significant drug enforcement activity. Charges involving cocaine, methamphetamine, heroin, fentanyl, and prescription drugs are common.
  • Assault Crimes: Assault and battery, aggravated assault, assault with a dangerous weapon, and assault with intent to do great bodily harm less than murder.
  • Retail Fraud: With numerous shopping centers and retail districts, shoplifting charges are extremely common in Kent County, particularly in Kentwood and Wyoming.
  • Weapons Charges: Carrying a concealed weapon (CCW), felony firearm, felon in possession, and other gun crimes.
  • Property Crimes: Breaking and entering, home invasion, larceny, motor vehicle theft, and receiving stolen property.
  • Traffic Offenses: Driving while license suspended (DWLS), reckless driving, and fleeing and eluding.
