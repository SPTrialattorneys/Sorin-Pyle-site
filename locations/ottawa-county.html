About the Ottawa County Criminal Justice System

Ottawa County is home to a robust criminal justice system that processes thousands of criminal cases each year. From traffic violations to serious felonies, the county's courts handle a wide range of criminal matters affecting residents and visitors throughout the region. Understanding how this system works is crucial when you're facing criminal charges.

The county's legal infrastructure serves a diverse population spanning from the lakeshore communities to the agricultural townships inland. Law enforcement agencies throughout Ottawa County work closely with prosecutors to build cases, making it essential to have experienced defense counsel who understands the local legal landscape and can effectively challenge the prosecution's evidence.

Ottawa County Courts: Where Your Case Will Be Heard

Your criminal case in Ottawa County will be heard in one of two court systems, depending on the severity of the charges:

20th Circuit Court - Grand Haven

Address: 414 Washington Street, Grand Haven, MI 49417

The 20th Circuit Court has jurisdiction over all felony cases in Ottawa County. This is where serious charges like assault with intent to do great bodily harm, drug trafficking, criminal sexual conduct, and other felonies are prosecuted. If your case goes to trial on felony charges, it will be heard by a jury in this courthouse. Our attorneys have extensive experience defending clients in the 20th Circuit Court and have built strong professional relationships with court personnel while maintaining our fierce advocacy for our clients.

58th District Court - Three Locations

The 58th District Court serves all of Ottawa County with three courthouse locations:

  • Grand Haven Division: 414 Washington Street, Grand Haven, MI 49417
  • Holland Division: 12120 Fillmore Street, West Olive, MI 49460
  • Hudsonville Division: 3100 Port Sheldon Street, Hudsonville, MI 49426

The 58th District Court handles all misdemeanor cases from arraignment through trial, as well as the preliminary stages of felony cases including arraignments and preliminary examinations. We regularly appear in all three divisions of this court and are familiar with the unique procedures and expectations at each location.

Common Criminal Charges in Ottawa County

Our firm defends clients against the full spectrum of criminal charges filed in Ottawa County. Some of the most common charges we handle include:

  • OWI/DUI Offenses: Operating while intoxicated, including first offense, second offense, and felony third offense cases. Ottawa County law enforcement agencies conduct regular sobriety checkpoints and patrol heavily for impaired drivers, particularly during summer months along the lakeshore.
  • Domestic Violence: These charges are prosecuted aggressively in Ottawa County. Even first-time offenses can carry serious consequences including jail time, probation, and firearms restrictions.
  • Drug Crimes: Possession, delivery, and manufacturing charges involving marijuana, cocaine, methamphetamine, prescription drugs, and other controlled substances.
  • Assault & Battery: Simple assault, aggravated assault, assault with a dangerous weapon, and assault with intent to do great bodily harm.
  • Theft Crimes: Retail fraud (shoplifting), larceny, embezzlement, and receiving stolen property.
  • Traffic Violations: Driving while license suspended, reckless driving, and other serious traffic offenses.

Local Law Enforcement in Ottawa County

Criminal charges in Ottawa County can originate from arrests made by various law enforcement agencies, including:

  • Ottawa County Sheriff's Department: Provides law enforcement services to townships and unincorporated areas throughout the county
  • Holland Department of Public Safety: Services for the City of Holland
  • Grand Haven Department of Public Safety: Serves the City of Grand Haven
  • Zeeland Police Department
  • Michigan State Police: Patrol highways and assist local agencies
  • Specialized Units: West Michigan Enforcement Team (WEMET) for drug investigations, crash reconstruction teams, and others

Each agency has its own procedures and practices. Our familiarity with these agencies helps us identify potential weaknesses in the prosecution's case and develop effective defense strategies.
