Common Criminal Charges in Zeeland, MI

Zeeland police conduct traffic enforcement along Main Avenue, State Street, and surrounding areas. DUI arrests often occur on weekends or after community events when residents are traveling home. Domestic Violence

Domestic violence arrests happen in every community, including Zeeland. Police must arrest someone when called to a domestic disturbance, even if the situation was a misunderstanding or blown out of proportion. Ottawa County prosecutes drug offenses aggressively, including marijuana possession (even small amounts), prescription drug violations, and more serious charges. Zeeland's youth population faces MIP charges from underage drinking at parties, school events, or local gatherings. These charges can affect college admissions, scholarships, and future employment. We help young people avoid convictions through diversion programs and reduced charges.

Assault charges can arise from disputes between neighbors, altercations at school functions, or confrontations that escalated unexpectedly. We defend clients by demonstrating self-defense, challenging witness credibility, and negotiating reduced charges.

What to Do If You're Arrested in Zeeland

If Zeeland police or Ottawa County Sheriff's deputies arrest you, follow these critical steps:

  1. Remain silent. Do NOT talk to police without a lawyer present. Invoke your right to remain silent and request an attorney immediately. Anything you say will be used against you.
  2. Do not consent to searches. Police may ask to search your car, phone, or home. Politely decline and say you do not consent to searches.
  3. Call an attorney immediately. The sooner we start working on your case, the better. Call us at (616) 227-3303.
  4. Do not post about your case on social media. In a small community like Zeeland, word spreads quickly. Prosecutors will review your social media accounts, and so will your neighbors. Don't post anything about your arrest or charges.
  5. Document everything. Write down what happened while it's fresh in your mind, including names of witnesses, photos of injuries, and any evidence supporting your defense.
