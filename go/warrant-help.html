Critical Warrant Information

üö® How to Check for Warrants

‚ñº

NEVER call police directly - they may arrest you!

Safe ways to check:

  • Have attorney check: We can verify without risk
  • Court website: Many counties post warrant lists online
  • Court clerk: Call and ask (don't give location)
  • OTIS system: Michigan offender tracking (for some warrants)

Red flags you may have a warrant:

  • Missed a court date
  • Stopped paying fines
  • Violated probation terms
  • Police came to your home/work looking for you
  • Got a letter about missing court

Without an Attorney:

üí∞ Bond & Bail Considerations

‚ñº

Factors affecting bond amount:

  • Seriousness of original charge
  • How long warrant has been active
  • Criminal history
  • Ties to community
  • Employment status
  • Whether you voluntarily surrendered

Types of release:

  • Personal Recognizance (PR): No money, just promise
  • Cash/Surety Bond: Must pay 10% or full amount
  • Conditional Bond: Released with conditions (testing, curfew)
  • Tether/GPS: Electronic monitoring instead of jail

We fight for lowest possible bond or PR release!

üìã Preparing to Surrender

‚ñº

‚úÖ Before Surrender Checklist:

  • Arrange childcare/pet care
  • Notify employer (may need day off)
  • Bring ID and any court papers
  • Wear appropriate court attire
  • Leave valuables at home
  • Bring prescription medications
  • Have bail money ready (if needed)
  • Arrange ride home from court
  • Turn off phone (will be confiscated)

What happens during surrender:

  • Meet attorney at courthouse
  • Process booking (fingerprints, photo)
  • Immediate appearance before judge
  • Attorney argues for release
  • Judge sets bond and conditions
  • Process release paperwork
  • Walk out with new court date

üöî If Police Come to Arrest You

‚ñº

If police show up BEFORE you surrender:

  • Stay calm and comply
  • Do NOT resist or run
  • Say: "I'm invoking my right to remain silent"
  • Say: "I want to speak to my attorney"
  • Do NOT explain or make excuses
  • Do NOT consent to searches
  • Call us immediately when able

They may come to:

  • Your home (early morning raids common)
  • Your workplace (extremely embarrassing)
  • Traffic stops (routine stops turn into arrests)
  • Any police contact (called for help, become arrested)

This is why voluntary surrender is so important!

‚ñº

Act IMMEDIATELY if:

  • Traveling soon (will be detained at airport)
