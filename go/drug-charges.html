Michigan Drug Charge Penalties

Use/Possession - Marijuana Up to 93 days jail + $2,000 fine (without medical card)
Use/Possession - Other Drugs Up to 1 year jail + $2,000 fine
Possession - Cocaine/Heroin Up to 4 years prison + $25,000 fine
Possession with Intent Up to 20 years prison + $25,000 fine
Delivery/Manufacturing Up to 20 years to LIFE + $1,000,000 fine

Critical Information for Drug Charges

üö® If Police Find Drugs

DO NOT admit ownership or make any statements!

  • Say NOTHING about the drugs
  • Don't explain why they're there
  • Don't claim they're someone else's

  • Request an attorney immediately

Say only: "I invoke my right to remain silent. I want to speak to my attorney."

Remember: "Constructive possession" means they can charge you even if drugs aren't on your person!

üîç Common Police Tactics

‚ñº

Be aware of these tactics used to build drug cases:

  • "It will be easier if you cooperate" - FALSE! Never helps you
  • "We'll go easy if you tell us who's dealing" - They can't make deals
  • "We already know everything" - If true, they wouldn't need you to talk
  • "Your friend already told us" - Classic lie to get confession
  • Controlled buys - Using informants to set you up

Remember: Police are trained to get confessions. Stay silent!

üöó Vehicle Searches for Drugs

‚ñº

Special rules apply to vehicle drug searches:

  • K9 units can sniff outside your car during a traffic stop
  • Alert from drug dog = probable cause to search
  • They CANNOT extend the stop unreasonably to wait for K9
  • Smell of marijuana may justify search (even with medical card)
  • Never consent to search - make them get warrant or use probable cause

Critical: Lock your car if asked to exit. Close all windows.

Say: "I do not consent to any searches."

‚öñÔ∏è Common Defenses

‚ñº

Experienced attorneys can challenge drug charges through:

  • Illegal search: Evidence thrown out if rights violated
  • Lack of possession: Drugs weren't yours or in your control
  • Entrapment: Police induced you to commit crime
  • Chain of custody: Evidence mishandled or contaminated
  • Lab errors: Testing mistakes or false positives
  • Medical marijuana: Valid card and compliance with law

Important: Every case is different. Get an attorney to review your specific situation.
