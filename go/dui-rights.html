Your Rights During a DUI Stop

üö® Michigan's Implied Consent Law

‚ñº

Critical: By driving in Michigan, you've already consented to chemical testing if arrested for DUI.

  • Refusing a chemical test after arrest = automatic 1-year license suspension
  • This is separate from any criminal penalties
  • You have the right to request an independent test at your expense
  • Officer must advise you of consequences before testing

Important: This applies to chemical tests AFTER arrest, not preliminary breath tests (PBT) during the stop.

üå¨Ô∏è Preliminary Breath Test (PBT)

‚ñº

The roadside breath test BEFORE arrest:

  • You CAN refuse the PBT (roadside test) in most cases
  • Refusal is a civil infraction: $150 fine + 2 points
  • PBT results cannot be used at trial (only for probable cause)
  • Exception: Drivers under 21 face harsher penalties for PBT refusal

Strategy: Politely decline if you've been drinking. The civil infraction is often better than providing evidence.

üö∂ Field Sobriety Tests

‚ñº

Physical coordination tests are VOLUNTARY in Michigan:

  • You can refuse ALL field sobriety tests without penalty
  • Common tests: walk-and-turn, one-leg stand, eye test (HGN)
  • These tests are subjective and often inaccurate
  • Medical conditions, anxiety, or weather can affect results
  • Your refusal cannot be used against you in court

Say politely: "Officer, I prefer not to perform field sobriety tests."

üí¨ What NOT to Say

‚ñº

Statements that hurt your case:

  • Never admit to drinking (even "just two beers")
  • Don't explain where you're coming from
  • Don't estimate when you last drank
  • Don't apologize or admit wrongdoing
  • Don't try to talk your way out of it

Safe response: "Officer, I prefer to remain silent and would like to speak with my attorney."

üì± Document Everything

‚ñº

Protect yourself by gathering evidence:

  • Record the stop if possible (legal in Michigan)
  • Note the exact location and time
  • Remember what you ate/drank and when
  • Document any medical conditions affecting balance
  • Save receipts from bars/restaurants
  • Get witness contact information

Critical: Request a copy of all videos from police within 30 days.

‚è∞ Critical Deadlines After Arrest

‚ñº

You MUST act quickly to protect your rights:

  • 14 DAYS: Request administrative hearing to contest license suspension
  • 30 DAYS: Request police video evidence
  • 45 DAYS: File motions to challenge evidence
  • IMMEDIATE: Contact an attorney before making ANY statements

Missing these deadlines waives important rights permanently!
