## MICHIGAN COLLEGE STUDENT CRIMINAL LAW OVERVIEW

### Key Legal Changes (2018)

**MIP Law Reform:**
- Effective January 1, 2018
- **First MIP offense = Civil infraction** (not misdemeanor)
- First offense fine: Up to $100 (varies by court)
- **Second offense = Misdemeanor** (remains criminal)
- Third offense = Enhanced penalties

### Common Charges & Penalties

**1. Minor in Possession (MIP) - MCL 436.1703**
- **First Offense:** Civil infraction, up to $100 fine
- **Second Offense:** Misdemeanor, up to 30 days jail, doubled fines
- **Third Offense:** Misdemeanor, up to 60 days jail, up to $500 fine
- **Bodily Alcohol Content (BAC):** Any BAC above 0.02 violates law

**2. Fake ID Offenses**
- **Possession/use of fake ID:** Misdemeanor
- **Penalties:** More severe than in the past due to business pressure
- **Consequences:** Police called, criminal charges filed (not just confiscation anymore)

**3. Open Intoxicant**
- **Violation:** Possessing open alcohol container in public, vehicles
- **Penalty:** Misdemeanor, fines, possible jail time

**4. Drug Possession**
- **Marijuana:** Misdemeanor or civil infraction depending on amount (under 21)
- **Prescription drugs without prescription:** Felony
- **Controlled substances:** Felony charges

**5. DUI/OWI for College Students**
- **Under 21 Zero Tolerance:** 0.02 BAC or higher = violation
- **Standard OWI:** 0.08 BAC = criminal charge
- **Penalties:** License suspension, jail time, fines, probation

### Enforcement Patterns

**University Police Collaboration:**
- Local police work closely with campus police
- Increased enforcement during:
  - Welcome Week/move-in
  - Homecoming
  - Spring break
  - Major sporting events
  - Warm weather weekends

**Common Enforcement Locations:**
- Off-campus house parties
- Bars and nightclubs (ID checks)
- Campus events
- Parking lots
- Walking home from parties (open intoxicant, public intoxication)

**Statistics:**
- U of M example: 48 tickets, 2 arrests in single underage drinking detail
- 46 MIP tickets, 2 open intoxicant tickets in one enforcement operation

### Long-Term Consequences for Students

**Academic Consequences:**
- Enrollment status affected
- Loss of on-campus housing
- Federal financial aid eligibility (drug convictions)
- Scholarship loss (conduct violations)
- Internship termination
- Research opportunities revoked

**Professional Consequences:**
- Background checks for internships, jobs
- Professional licensing (nursing, teaching, pharmacy, law, medicine)
- Graduate school admissions
- Study abroad program disqualification
- Athletic eligibility
- Security clearances (government, defense industry)

**Personal Consequences:**
- Parental notification
- Family disappointment
- Reputation damage in small campus communities
- Relationship problems
- Mental health impact (anxiety, depression)
