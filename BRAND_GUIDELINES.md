# Sorin & Pyle Brand Guidelines

## Brand Identity

**Sorin & Pyle, PC** is a criminal defense law firm based in West Michigan, founded by Sorin Panainte (first-generation immigrant, former public defender) and Jonathan Pyle (West Michigan native).

### Brand Positioning
- Judgment-free trial lawyers
- Focus on second chances and client empowerment
- Unapologetically human approach to criminal defense
- Specialization in cases involving mental health and substance abuse

### Core Values
- **Empathy First** - "We give a damn!"
- **Second Chances** - Especially for juveniles
- **Inner Peace** - "Everyone deserves the best and to find their inner peace"
- **Client-Centered** - Advocacy over technicality

## Visual Identity

### Typography

The firm uses premium fonts for print materials and free web-safe alternatives for digital platforms.

#### Print/Premium Typefaces

**Headers:**
- **Baskerville URW Bold**
- Classic serif with warmth and authority
- Use for print materials, business cards, signage, and formal documents
- **License Required:** Commercial license from URW Type Foundry (~$20-35)

**Body (Print):**
- **Gotham Book**
- Clean, modern, highly legible
- Use for printed materials, presentations, and marketing collateral
- **License Required:** Commercial license from Hoefler & Co. (~$177+ per user)

#### Web/Digital Typefaces (Free Alternatives)

**Headers (Web):**
- **Lora** (Bold/Regular)
- Free Google Font - elegant serif similar to Baskerville
- Use for website headings, email templates, and digital documents
- **License:** Open source (SIL Open Font License)

**Body (Web):**
- **Inter** (Regular/Medium)
- Free Google Font - modern sans-serif similar to Gotham
- Use for website body copy, digital communications
- **License:** Open source (SIL Open Font License)

#### Typographic Hierarchy

**Print Materials:**
```
H1: Baskerville URW Bold, Large
H2: Baskerville URW Bold, Medium
H3: Baskerville URW Bold, Small
Body: Gotham Book, Regular
Subtext: Gotham Book, Small
```

**Web/Digital:**
```
H1: Lora Bold, Large
H2: Lora Bold, Medium
H3: Lora Regular, Small
Body: Inter Regular
Subtext: Inter Regular, Small
```

## Logo System

### Primary Logo
The primary logo features the sailing ship icon with "Sorin & Pyle" wordmark and "TRIAL LAWYERS" tagline.

**Formats Available:**
- Primary Horizontal Layout
- Stacked Vertical Layout
- Logo Only (wordmark without ship)
- Icon Only (ship with S&P monogram)

### Logo Variations

#### Color Versions
- Full color (blue ship, orange text accents)
- Single color (all blue or all white)
- White on color backgrounds
- Color on white backgrounds

### Logo Usage Guidelines

**Minimum Clear Space:**
- Maintain clear space around logo equal to the height of the "S" in "Sorin"

**Minimum Size:**
- Horizontal: 200px wide (digital), 2" wide (print)
- Stacked: 150px wide (digital), 1.5" wide (print)
- Icon only: 80px (digital), 0.75" (print)

**Do Not:**
- Distort or stretch the logo
- Change the colors
- Rotate the logo
- Add effects (shadows, outlines, gradients)
- Place on busy backgrounds that reduce legibility

## Brand Symbolism

### The Sailing Ship Icon

The ship represents:
- **Navigation** - Guiding clients through turbulent legal waters
- **Journey** - The legal process as a voyage toward resolution
- **Safe Harbor** - Bringing clients to safety and inner peace
- **Strength & Stability** - Experience and reliability in rough seas
- **Michigan Heritage** - Maritime connection to West Michigan roots
- **S&P Monogram** - Orange initials on the sails create memorable brand mark

This metaphor aligns with the firm's mission of guiding clients through difficult times to find peace and second chances.

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Voice Characteristics
- **Direct & Conversational** - Speak plainly, avoid legal jargon
- **Unapologetic** - Confident in standing up for clients
- **Human** - Real, authentic, judgment-free

### Tone Guidelines

**Do:**
- Use active voice and clear language
- Show empathy and understanding
- Be honest and transparent
- Focus on client needs and outcomes
- Emphasize hope and second chances

**Don't:**
- Use excessive legal jargon or Latin phrases
- Sound corporate or cold
- Make promises you can't keep
- Judge or moralize
- Sound overly formal or stuffy

### Messaging Examples

**Taglines:**
- "Trial Lawyers"
- "We give a damn!"
- "Judgment-free"
- "Everyone deserves the best and to find their inner peace"
